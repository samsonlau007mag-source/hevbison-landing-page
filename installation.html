Introduction to Toyota Hybrid Third-party Battery (HEVBISON)

Standard Operating Procedure

Installation Precautions - Standard Operating Procedure (Precautions, i.e., Warranty Prerequisites)

Step 1 Step 2 Step 3 Step 4 Step 5 Step 6
1
Preparation

Vehicle Information Collection

Before removing the original old battery pack from the vehicle, the following tasks must be completed:

1.1 Vehicle Information

Vehicle model and year, VIN, license plate, instrument cluster display Mileage, serial number range of the installed battery, and installation outlet information.

——— Purpose: To provide feedback to the original manufacturer for warranty registration and activation of a valid warranty card.

1.2 Photography

Old MPG data displayed on the instrument cluster (including corresponding speed in MPH).

——— Purpose: To compare the MPG before and after replacing the new battery.

2
Verification

Confirmation of the Positive and Negative Poles at the Starting Position of the Old Battery Pack

Mark the electrode polarity of the two terminals of the old battery pack on the BMS backplane.

3
Replacement

Replace with New Wiring Harness Copper Sheets

Wiring Harness Copper Sheets must be replaced with the brand-new copper sheets included by HEVBISON.

  • Old copper sheets will always have oxidation. The sanding process cannot ensure flatness.
  • Especially for a vehicle where the hybrid battery has been repaired before, the copper sheets have become extremely uneven and inconsistent in thickness after multiple previous sandings. After tightening the screw, full contact with the battery electrode cannot be guaranteed, which will reduce electrical conductivity and increase impedance and heat generation.
  • After replacing with brand-new copper sheets, photos should be taken and sent back to the original manufacturer or authorized distributor.
  • Old copper sheets need to be recycled and returned to the original manufacturer or authorized distributor along with the old battery cells.
4
Disassembly

Two Battery Cell Disassembly Methods and Precautions

4.1 Method 1

Simply remove the outward-facing end baffle and slide the battery cell out.

4.2 Method 2

Remove the entire battery set along with the bracket from the BMS.

Installation Precautions:

  • After replacing with new battery cells, do not tighten the end screws of the 4 black tie rods all at once; just ensure they do not fall off. This leaves some room for the battery cells to move, facilitating alignment and locking between the battery cells and the BMS backplane.
  • Lift the entire battery set onto the BMS backplane and tighten the bottom screw of each battery cell into the M5 screw holes with a maximum torque of 4.5Nm (the torque for the baffle screws at both terminals can be slightly higher; manual tightening is recommended).
  • Finally, tighten the black tie rod screws completely; manual tightening is recommended. The torque of an electric wrench is too high and can easily cause excessive compression of the battery cells or even deformation of the housing.
5
Fixing

A Screw Must Be Installed at the Bottom of Each Battery Cell

  • A fixing screw must be tightened at the bottom of every battery cell. This prevents the battery from sliding in the direction of inertia during vehicle acceleration or sudden braking.
  • The fixation of the battery cell primarily relies on the bottom screw, followed by the structural interlocking between the battery cells, and finally the auxiliary fixation provided by the tie rod and the two end baffles.

Each battery cell has only one screw hole at the bottom corresponding to the battery positive terminal and the hole position on the BMS backplane, allowing for only one installation direction. Fixing the battery cell to the BMS backplane also serves as a process to double-check whether the battery cell electrode orientation is correct.

6
Torque

Torque Range and Precautions for Battery Cell Body Connection Screws

  • For all screws connected to the battery cell (such as wiring harness terminal post screws and battery cell bottom fastening screws), the maximum torque must not exceed 3.5Nm.
  • The aforementioned screws should be tightened using a calibrated wrench with torque measurement (a setting of 2.5Nm is recommended); the manufacturer will provide an electric screwdriver with the appropriate torque value for installation.

Consequences of Non-Compliance

Screws connected to the battery cell must not be tightened using an electric wrench. Doing so may cause the electrode to break or even cause the battery casing to crack, damaging the internal electrode wires of the battery.
